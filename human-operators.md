# Human Operators — Principal & Managing Consultant

NAUCode operates as a 2-desk consulting + orchestration model. Two humans are
the only operators with decision authority. All AI workers across all
companies report to one or both of them.

**Faces outward to:** C-level, board, partners, key accounts
**Reports to:** self / advisory board

### Owns

- Strategy (vision, market positioning, revenue model)

## Managing Consultant

**Name:** TODO — fill in
**Faces outward to:** Middle management, vendors, operational stakeholders
**Reports to:** Principal (on strategy), self (on operations)

### Owns

- Delivery — engagement execution end-to-end
- Vendor / fellow team relationship management
- P&L tracking per engagement + cross-company roll-up
- Operational deals (deal value < AUD 20,000)
- SLA enforcement
- Quality assurance of fellow team deliverables before client sees them

### Quality gates Managing MUST review

- Deliverable sign-off before sending to client (`engagement-*/output/`)
- Vendor PO < AUD 20,000
- Fellow team scorecard monthly review (`vendor-*/scorecard-current.md`)
- Engagement kickoff agenda
- Anything tagged `delivery` or `vendor` in incoming routing

### Time allocation (target Phase 1)

- 70% operations (engagement coordination + vendor management)
- 30% client management (middle-mgmt stakeholders)

## Both must review (joint quality gates)

- Proposal between AUD 20,000 — AUD 50,000
- New fellow team onboarding (MSA + scorecard baseline)
- Engagement post-mortem with > 1 surprise
- IP licensing where licensee is also a delivery client
- Annual budget + IP portfolio review

## Anti-overlap rules

1. Principal **does not** review delivery deliverables before client. Managing's job.
2. Managing **does not** sign proposals ≥ AUD 50,000 or commit IP licensing terms.
3. Both attend ≤ 1 meeting per enterprise account per quarter — split coverage otherwise.
4. If a decision falls in a grey zone, Managing escalates within 24h. Principal
   responds within 48h. No silent ambiguity.

## Out-of-scope (escalate or refuse)

Both operators decline tasks that fall outside their scope and the company
charter. Examples:

- Tax filing → external accountant (referenced in `compliance/`)
- Patent prosecution → external IP counsel (referenced in `compliance/`)
- Litigation strategy → external counsel
- Industry-licensed work (e.g., audit signing, legal advice) → not offered
